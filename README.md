# 👥 HR Analytics Dashboard — Power BI

> An interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing employee 
attrition, department trends, salary impact, and 
workforce demographics across 1,417 employees.

---

## 📌 Overview
This project presents a comprehensive HR Analytics 
Dashboard built in Power BI to help HR teams identify 
attrition patterns and take data-driven decisions on 
employee retention.

---

## ❓ Problem Statement
Organizations struggle to identify why employees leave 
and which departments, salary slabs, or age groups are 
most at risk. This dashboard provides a unified view of 
attrition trends to support strategic HR decisions.

---

## 📂 Dataset
- **Records:** 1,417 employees
- **Fields Used:** Department, Age Group, Gender, Salary 
  Slab, Job Role, Experience, Job Satisfaction, 
  Attrition Status

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technology
| Tool | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Power BI | Dashboard development & visualization |
| DAX | Calculated measures & KPIs |
| Power Query | Data cleaning & transformation |

---

## 💡 Key Insights
- 📉 Overall attrition rate is **16.3%** (231 out of 1,417)
- 💼 **Sales department** has the highest attrition at 36.36%
- 💰 Employees earning **6–10 LPA** show highest attrition (399)
- 👶 **26–35 age group** has the most employees (588)
- 👩 **Female attrition (36.8%)** is lower than male attrition
- 🔬 **Laboratory Technicians** have the highest attrition by job role

---

## ✅ Results & Conclusion
The dashboard reveals that attrition is highest among 
mid-salary employees in Sales and Operations departments. 
Early-career employees (26–35 age group) are most likely 
to leave, suggesting the need for better engagement and 
growth opportunities for junior staff.
